Message from the founder
When I think about my "WHY"...It definitely makes me want to cry.  I founded Brothers Building up Brothers (B.B.B.) in 2004-2005 at Tri Cities High School as a result of watching males, of various backgrounds, wander the halls like vagabonds.  Initially, I had no idea about what to do or how to do; nonetheless, I knew "WHY" I must do something. 

If we fast-forward to 2007-2008 at Therrell High School, I saw a similar trend, and I dedicated myself to changing this negative image by recruiting and developing young men who would shape a new course for our community's future.  Brothers Building up Brothers (B.B.B.) is one of the most visible organizations in APS, and it consistently produces scholars who are attending America’s top institutions of higher learning. 

The young men of B.B.B. are changing the way their peers view the idea of being smart.  Moreover, they are dedicated to building up the community and inspiring the next generation by keeping their eyes on the fraternity's aims: Scholarship, Leadership, and Stewardship.  I merely serve as a sounding board, griot, and guide who is determined to see this organization continue to grow and produce successful young men who will forever hold high the banner of B.B.B.  I endeavor to see chapters erected on campuses and at community centers throughout the state and nation in the future by encouraging my little brothers, as well as myself, to always remain loyal to our mission, vision, and spirit!!!!!

Message from the President

As Dr. King states, "we all must accept finite disappointments but never lose infinite hope." This statement carries through me and the organization showing that we know that we are all going to have bumps in the road but its our jobs to pick each other up and encourage each other to keep on going. As brothers, we will diminish all the stereotypes and hatred towards African-American brothers and our youth using One Mission, One Vision, and One Spirit.
